Education in India is a Concurrent List subject, that is both the Indian central government, and the state governments have responsibility for enacting and implementing education policy.[34] The central board and most of the state boards uniformly follow the "10+2" pattern of education.[35]: 3  In this pattern, study of 10 years is done in schools and 2 years in Junior colleges (Maharashtra) or Higher Secondary Schools(most other states),[35]: 44  and then 3 years of study for a bachelor's degree.[36] The first 10 years is further subdivided into 8 years of elementary education (5 years Primary School and 3 years Middle School), 2 years of Secondary education followed by 2 years of Higher Secondary Schools or Junior colleges.[35]: 5  This pattern originated from the recommendation of the Education Commission of 1964–66.[37]
